case study

Creating the interactive applications were both a pain and a blessing for my career as a designer. Not only was I still very unfamiliar with HTML at the time, but I had various projects from other classes weighing me down.

To begin with these projects, I drew various wireframe layouts and sketched some artwork that I could use on them. For instance, when working on the Bees in Peril application, I began with the question: what elements capture the essence of bees? What I then decided to add were images of bees, honeycombs, and flowers. I then decided on using illustrations as the main imagery. After that, I simply used Photoshop to create and implement the artwork.

Once I found my stride, these projects made me realize the potential on how creative we can be through web design. The touchscreen gesture app gave me insight on the fact that the applications that we were making in that class are of the same nature as everyday objects like the Coca-Cola Freestyle machines, or the interactive displays at the museum. The gesture showed me how creative I could be with the type of inputs that I use to operate these applications.
